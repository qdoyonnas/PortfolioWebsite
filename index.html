Welcome to my portfolio!

My name is Quentin Doyonnas and I am a video game developer. I have loved video games for as long as I can remember. I have always had a great passion for the stories they tell, the emotions they evoke, and how they connect people who otherwise might have nothing in common. When I was 10 years old, I decided I would spend my life making games. Then at 12 years old I taught myself some, rudimentary, Python programming and wrote my first game, Destroyer Command.

From there I continued to explore games, learned more programming taking elective classes and extracurricular courses. I wrote game docs, filling notebooks and hard drives and tried where I could to realize my dreams. I made more games in my spare time, and eventually started my higher education in the same pursuit. Some years later, I graduated with a Bachelor's of Science in Video Game Development.

Here are some of the projects I have made over the course of my education and career. Some of these projects were the result of assignments and many were the result of personal motivations

Incantorium
▼
▼

Incantorium was my first VR project. The player character is trapped in a study-like room and must attempt to escape using magic they learn. While the tutorial aspects of the game were not completed, the room has a couple puzzles to solve in order to open the final door and escape. Most props can be interacted with and munipulated in VR. This includes being able to pick up and throw objects, lighting candles by touching wicks together and extinguishing candles by making a pinching motion on the wicks. Most models and art were done by hand.

  • Unity Engine
  • Oculus Rift and Steam VR project
  • Puzzle Solving
  • Escape Room
  • Gesture and prop based magic system

Epic Punch Through
▼
▼

Epic Punch Through is an on going passion project. It is the first installment of a series of games in the V-Souls universe. Each game is planned to be more expansive and ambitions than the last. EPT is merely a score attack game while the sequels will have RPG mechanics and over-world style travelling, as well as unique storylines.   • Robust architecture allowing effecient work flow

N.A.C OS
▼
▼

Started during a game jam themed around connection, N.A.C OS is a hacking simulator. It simulates an abstract network of nodes. Players are tasked with exploring this network and disconnecting their rivals. They do this by entering commands into a console, which are then interpreted and executed. With this players can write simple scripts inside the node to serve as automated programs for attack and defense.

  • Unity Engine
  • Text-parsing using regular expressions
  • Automated camera using tweening
  • Text to number conversion system to allow any word to be evaluated as a unique number

We are not the Fallen
▼
▼

We are not the Fallen was a quick side project made functional in under a week. Players use their powerful jumps and the physics of falling debris in order to climb as high as possible. Collisions between debris cause powerful screen shake and particle explosions which can catch the unwary player, ending their run.

  • Unity Engine
  • Physics based gameplay
  • Addicting gameplay-loop
  • High-performance 4-directional endless generation

TumbleLife
▼
▼

Made in the space of a few days as an alternative assignment for an art class. TumbleLife sees players stacking blocks using physics on a small platform. The amount of time the player can 'keep it altogether' is counted as score. Blocks are contrained to work on a 2d plane though the game is presented in three dimensions.

  • Unity Engine
  • Physics based interaction
  • Displays rigid physics and contraints
  • Improved Unity physics collision detection to avoid glitches
  • Complete gameplay loop

Genetic Algorithm Terrain Generator
▼
▼

Simple terrain generator using a genetic algorithm. The program is provided with a number of tiles (sand, forest, water, ice etc) and certain rules on what the world should be composed of. The GA will then attempt to generate a world that matches these criteria. Educational project on the subject of GA and what to use, or not use, them in.

  • Genetic Algorithm
  • Terrain Generation
  • Windows Forms and .NET interface
